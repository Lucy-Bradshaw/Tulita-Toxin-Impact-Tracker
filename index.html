What are phthalates in fragrance?
Diethyl phthalate (DEP) and related esters are used as fixatives in synthetic fragrances. They penetrate skin and are inhaled. Land et al. (2025) confirmed them as endocrine-disrupting chemicals measurable in urine within hours.
Hormonal disruption mechanism
Phthalates interfere with androgen and estrogen receptor pathways — Land et al. (2025) link urinary metabolites to reduced testosterone and altered estrogen signalling. Hermosa (2016) shows dermal absorption bypasses first-pass metabolism, raising EDC bioavailability.
Cumulative daily exposure burden
Hermosa (2016) found personal care products contribute disproportionately to daily EDC exposure — 4–25 µg/kg/day in high-use scenarios, near or above EFSA tolerable intake.
Why switching matters
Tulita's 100% plant-based, phthalate-free formulations remove this pathway entirely — an estimated 730–5,475 µg/day-equivalent exposures avoided per switcher each year.
